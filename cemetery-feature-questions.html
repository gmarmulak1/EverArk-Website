What is Cemetery Software Inventory Mapping & Management?
Cemetery inventory and mapping refers to the process of cataloging and visualizing all burial plots, structures, and related features within a cemetery. It serves as the foundation for effective cemetery management, helping staff keep track of available and occupied plots, burial records, and overall land use.

What does Cemetery Inventory Mapping and Management include:
​
1. Cemetery Inventory
This is the database side of cemetery management — a detailed record of everything within the cemetery, including:
  • Grave plots (location, dimensions, status: sold/available/interred)
  • Burials (deceased person’s name, date of burial, plot ID)
  • Ownership records (deeds, purchase history)
  • Structures (columbariums, mausoleums, monuments)
  • Assets (benches, signage, irrigation systems, roads, trees)

2. Cemetery Mapping
This is the visual or geospatial representation of the cemetery layout, often linked directly to inventory records. Mapping may include:
  • Plot layout maps (grids or sections showing all plots)
  • GIS/GPS mapping (plots georeferenced using latitude/longitude)
  • Interactive maps (used by staff or the public to locate graves)
  • Overlay maps (PDFs or KML files laid on Google Earth)

What Cemetery Tools are used:
  • Software platforms like EverArk
  • GIS tools like ArcGIS, Google Earth, or QGIS
  • GPS devices or drones for accurate positioning
  • Databases like Excel, Access, or cloud tools like Supabase/PostgreSQL

Why does it matter:
  • Prevents double-selling plots
  • Makes it easy to locate graves
  • Aids in space planning and development
  • Enhances transparency and service for families
  • Enables digital transformation and legacy preservation

What is Cemetery Software Sales and Deal Tracking?
Cemetery sales and deals tracking in software refers to how cemetery management platforms help track the sale of burial plots, services, and memorial products — along with managing contracts, payment plans, and customer interactions. It’s essentially the CRM plus a sales ledger tailored to the unique needs of a cemetery.

What does Cemetery Sales software cover:

1. Plot Sales Management
  • Tracks available, reserved, sold, or pre-need plots
  • Links sales to specific locations on the cemetery map
  • Manages plot pricing tiers (premium, standard, etc.)

2. Contracts & Deals
Creates digital contracts for:
  • Plot purchases
  • Burial services
  • Headstones or markers
  • Tracks deal stages: inquiry → quote → contract → payment → fulfilled

3. Payment Tracking
Logs payment history, including:
  • Down payments
  • Monthly installments
  • Final balances
  • Tracks financing terms, due dates, and automates reminders

4. Sales CRM
  • Stores contact info and purchase history for each family or customer
  • Notes follow-ups, calls, and communications
  • Supports lead tracking and pre-need planning pipelines

5. Reporting & Analytics
Shows reports like:
  • Total sales by month/year
  • Available vs sold plots
  • Revenue from products/services
  • Sales rep performance tracking (if applicable)

What are common features in Cemetery Software like EverArk:
  • Interactive Map Select and sell plots visually
  • Digital Contracts Auto-generate agreements
  • Payment Portal Take and track payments online
  • Sales Dashboard Monitor performance and sales stats
  • Pre-Need Sales Workflow Manage long-term customer relationships

What is Cemetery Software Customer Mangement?
Cemetery Customer Management is the process of tracking and managing all interactions, relationships, and transactions with cemetery clients — including families, funeral homes, and service providers — using a centralized system, often part of cemetery software.

It’s basically a CRM (Customer Relationship Management) system designed specifically for the cemetery and deathcare industry.

Who Are “Customers” in a Cemetery Context?
  • Families purchasing plots (at-need or pre-need)
  • Individuals arranging for cremation, burial, or memorials
  • Funeral homes coordinating services
  • Monument companies or florists
  • Churches or community groups

What does Cemetery Customer Management include:

1. Contact Information
  • Names, phone numbers, emails
  • Mailing addresses
  • Emergency contacts
  • Relationship to the deceased

2. Communication History
  • Notes from phone calls, emails, or in-person visits
  • Automated follow-ups and appointment reminders
  • Outreach for anniversaries, holidays, or pre-need updates

3. Sales & Services Records
  • Burial plot and product purchases
  • Payment history and financing status
  • Service history (funeral, interment, cremation)

4. Document Management
  • Contracts, deeds, permits
  • Memorial preferences
  • Digital signatures and uploaded files

5. Task & Workflow Tracking
  • Assign tasks to staff for client follow-ups
  • Track stages: lead → opportunity → customer → aftercare

Why is Cemetery Customer Management important:
  • Avoids lost info or duplicate records
  • Provides better service to grieving families
  • Improves pre-need sales and follow-up opportunities
  • Enables marketing and outreach
  • Centralizes access for all staff and departments

What are features in Cemetery CRM Software:
  • Customer Profiles All records in one place
  • Automated Follow-Ups Consistent communication
  • Contract Storage Quick access to documents
  • Call/Email Logs Accountability and transparency
  • Reports Understand sales and service trends

What is Cemetery Software Order Management?
Cemetery software order management is the system cemeteries use to create, track, fulfill, and manage orders for all the products and services they sell — like burial plots, markers, vaults, flowers, and memorial services — all within a digital platform.

It's like a point-of-sale (POS) and fulfillment system designed specifically for cemeteries.

What do "Orders" typically include:
  • Plot purchases (single, double, family lots)
  • Cremation niches or mausoleum crypts
  • Headstones, markers, vases
  • Burial services (opening/closing graves)
  • Memorial services (setups for ceremonies)
  • Maintenance services (plot care, flower placement)

What does Cemetery Order Management Do:
  • Create Orders Staff generate an order based on the customer's purchase (plots, services, products)
  • Track Orders Status moves from "Available", "Hold", "Pending", to "Sold"
  • Link to Payments Orders connect directly to invoices, payment plans, or receipts
  • Generate Paperwork Contracts, work orders for grounds crew, burial permits
  • Monitor Order Status Alerts if services are delayed, paid, or overdue

Why does Order Management matter in a Cemetery:
  • Reduces Errors: Keeps track of multiple moving parts like payment, burial scheduling, and stone installation
  • Improves Customer Experience: Families get faster, more organized service
  • Increases Revenue: Helps upsell memorials, services, or maintenance plans
  • Streamlines Internal Operations: Office staff, sales, and grounds crew all stay in sync

What are Key Features of Cemetery Order Management Software:
  • Digital Order Forms: Quickly create custom invoices
  • Order Status Tracking: See where each invoice is at a glance
  • Invoice & Payment Integration Automatically generates billing
  • Notifications Alerts: Warnings for past due invoices
  • Reporting: Analyze order trends and revenue by product/service

What is Cemetery Software Operations Management?
Cemetery software operations management refers to how cemetery management platforms help coordinate, organize, and streamline all the daily tasks, workflows, and services that keep a cemetery running smoothly — beyond just sales or mapping.

It’s basically the operational nerve center of a cemetery: handling burials, maintenance, grounds crew tasks, scheduling, compliance, and records.

​What does Cemetery Operations Management cover:
  • Burial Scheduling: Plan grave openings, cremations, memorial services, staff assignments
  • Grounds Maintenance: Schedule and track mowing, repairs, plot care, snow removal
  • Work Order Management: Assign tasks (dig graves, set markers, repair roads, trim trees)

What are typical Features in Cemetery Operations Management Software:
  • Digital Calendar: Schedule burials, services, and maintenance tasks
  • Automated Workflows: Trigger tasks automatically based on services
  • Work Order System: Assign and track completion of operational tasks
  • Asset and Grounds Tracking: Map and monitor trees, benches, roads, irrigation
  • Dashboard & Reporting: Live view of operational performance

Why does Cemetery Operations Management matter:
  • Keeps services on schedule (no missed burials or double-bookings)
  • Improves staff coordination (grounds, admin, sales all work from the same info)
  • Reduces operational mistakes (like digging in the wrong plot)
  • Supports compliance (important for state, local, and religious regulations)
  • Extends asset life (proactive maintenance planning)

Cemetery operations management software makes sure that everything actually happens — correctly, legally, and on time — from digging the right grave to maintaining the grounds.

What is Cemetery Software Record Management?
Cemetery software record management is the system cemeteries use to create, organize, store, retrieve, and maintain all critical documents and data related to their operations — digitally, safely, and accessibly.

It’s like the digital filing cabinet for everything the cemetery is legally and operationally required to keep — but much faster, more organized, and often linked to maps, sales, and operations.

​What are examples of Cemetery Records:
  • Burial Records: Deceased’s name, dates of birth/death, burial date, plot ID
  • Plot Ownership Records: Deeds, contracts, resale history, rights of interment
  • Sales & Financial Records: Invoices, payment history, pricing sheets
  • Work Orders & Service Records: Maintenance logs, grave opening/closing reports
  • Legal and Compliance Records: Burial permits, regulatory filings, cemetery licenses
  • Correspondence and Agreements: Emails, signed contracts, service orders
  • Maps and Plot Layouts: Traditional maps, GPS-integrated maps, section diagrams

What are common features of Cemetery Record Management Software:
  • Centralized Record Storage: All documents and records linked in one secure place
  • Advanced Search: Quickly find records by name, date, plot number, or keywords
  • Digital Forms and Documents: Electronic contracts, burial permits, pre-need applications
  • Record Linking: Tie together a customer, their plot, their payments, and burial details automatically
  • Access Controls & Permissions: Control who can see or edit records (essential for compliance)
  • Cloud Backup: Ensure critical data isn’t lost to disasters or technical failures
  • Deceased/Inactive Record Management: Archive or flag old records for historical purposes

Why is Cemetery Record Management critical:
  • Ensures compliance with laws (states often require detailed, permanent burial records)
  • Preserves historical and genealogical data for families and researchers
  • Reduces paper clutter and saves space
  • Speeds up customer service (no more digging through filing cabinets)
  • Protects against loss from disasters (fire, flood, theft)
  • Makes audits easy (internal or government audits require fast, accurate record retrieval)

Cemetery record management software turns hundreds of years of vital documents into an organized, searchable, secure digital system, ensuring nothing is lost — and everything is easily accessible.

What is Cemetery Software Reporting and Dashboards?
Cemetery software reporting and dashboards are the tools that help cemetery managers see, understand, and act on key information about the cemetery’s operations, sales, customers, finances, and activities — all in real-time, visual formats.

It’s basically the control panel that turns raw data into clear insights you can use to make smarter decisions.

​What does Cemetery Reporting and Dashboards cover:
  • Burial & Interment Tracking: Number of burials by month, type of burials (full body, cremation)
  • Plot Sales Performance: Plots sold vs available, revenue from plot sales, top-selling sections
  • Operations & Work Orders: Completed vs pending services, average time to close work orders
  • Financial Reporting: Revenue, unpaid balances, payment plan statuses, aged receivables
  • Customer Tracking: Number of new leads, pre-need vs at-need customers, customer service metrics
  • Inventory Management: Available plots, inventory of vaults, niches, and memorial products

What are common Features of Cemetery Reporting & Dashboards:
  • Visual Dashboards: See live updates with charts, graphs, KPIs (key performance indicators)
  • Customizable Reports: Filter by date, product, staff, section, service type, etc.
  • Scheduled Reports: Automatically email reports to managers weekly, monthly, etc.
  • Export Options: Download reports in PDF, Excel, or CSV formats
  • Trend Analysis: Compare current vs past performance to spot trends
  • Alerts and Notifications: Set thresholds for alerts (e.g., inventory running low, overdue payments)

Why does Cemetery Reporting and Dashboards matter:
  • Catch problems early (like falling sales or maintenance backlogs)
  • Plan better (forecast plot availability, staff needs, or cash flow)
  • Improve accountability (track staff work and service turnaround)
  • Support decision-making (invest in needed areas like marketing or grounds)
  • Prove compliance (easy audits and transparent reporting for regulators)

Cemetery software reporting and dashboards transform daily operations into easy-to-understand visuals, helping managers run smarter, faster, and more profitably.

What is Cemetery Software Administration?
Cemetery software administration is the setup, configuration, control, and overall management of the cemetery’s digital system — making sure the software runs properly, securely, and matches the cemetery’s real-world operations.

It’s like being the IT manager + business process manager specifically for the cemetery’s software environment.

​What does Cemetery Software Administration involve:
  • System Setup: Configuring the cemetery layout (sections, plots, mausoleums, niches) in the software
  • User Management: Adding/removing staff accounts, setting permissions (who can view, edit, delete)
  • Data Management: Importing historical records, cleaning data, fixing duplicates
  • Customization: Adjusting settings for pricing, sales processes, reports, workflows, field properties and language
  • Security & Compliance: Managing data security, backups, user access controls, audit trails
  • Template and Form Management: Setting up templates for contracts, invoices, deeds, and burial permits
  • Workflow Management: Building task flows (e.g., burial prep automatically creates a work order)
  • Support & Troubleshooting: Handling technical issues, user training, liaising with the software vendor if needed

Why is Cemetery Software Administration important:
  • Ensures accuracy (the plots and records in the system match the cemetery’s real-world inventory)
  • Protects sensitive information (customer data, burial permits, payment info)
  • Keeps operations running smoothly (staff always know where to find what they need)
  • Supports growth (easy to add new sections, users, or service offerings)
  • Meets legal requirements (proper record-keeping and audit trails)

What are Key Tasks an Administrator would typically handle:
  • Configuring Plot Maps: Uploading a map and defining sections/plots with GPS data
  • Managing User Access: Giving grounds crew limited access while giving sales full customer access
  • Setting Up Forms: Creating templates for new customer contracts or sales receipts
  • Enforcing Security Policies: Setting password rules and two-factor authentication
  • Analyzing Data: Choosing which KPIs and reports to manage and leverage
  • Data Import & Cleanup: Migrating from spreadsheets or paper files into clean, digital records

Cemetery software administration is about keeping the digital system organized, secure, and customized to match how the cemetery actually operates — enabling the staff to serve families better and work more efficiently.

What is a Cemetery Software eCommerce Site?
A cemetery software eCommerce site is a website connected to the cemetery’s management system that allows customers to browse, select, and purchase cemetery products and services online — just like shopping on Amazon, but for burial plots, cremation niches, memorials, and related services.

It brings self-service and digital convenience to an industry that has traditionally been very manual and paper-driven.

What can be Sold Through a Cemetery eCommerce Site?
  • Burial Spaces: Plots, crypts, niches (pre-need or immediate need)
  • Services: Grave opening/closing, cremations, memorial services
  • Memorial Products: Headstones, markers, vases, urns, bronze plaques
  • Add-Ons: Floral placements, wreaths, benches, trees
  • Payment Plans: Pre-need contracts with financing options

What are Key Features of a Cemetery eCommerce Site:
  • Plot/Inventory Listings: Interactive map or catalog showing available plots and prices
  • Online Shopping Cart: Customers can select and buy plots, products, and services
  • Secure Payment Processing: Take credit cards, ACH, or financing payments
  • Digital Contracts & E-Signatures: Sign deeds and agreements online
  • Search & Filter Tools Find available spaces by section, price, or memorial type
  • Scheduling Allow customers to request burial dates or service appointments
  • CRM and Sales Integration Automatically update customer records and sales in the cemetery software
  • Order Management: Fulfill product orders (like headstones) automatically from online purchases

Why does a Cemetery eCommerce Site matter:
  • Offers convenience: Families can shop, plan, and pay from home — no need for in-person visits during stressful times.
  • Boosts pre-need sales: Sell plots and services to customers planning ahead.
  • Expands market reach: Out-of-town families can arrange burials or purchase memorials without traveling.
  • Improves cash flow: Instant online payments and financing can speed up sales cycles.
  • Modernizes the cemetery’s brand: Positions the cemetery as accessible, professional, and tech-forward.

A cemetery eCommerce site makes it possible to sell burial spaces, services, and memorial products online, giving families an easier experience while increasing revenue for the cemetery.

What is a Celebration of Life app?
A celebration of life app is a mobile or web application designed to help families and friends plan, personalize, and share a memorial event that honors a loved one’s life. It focuses on creating a positive, story-driven tribute rather than just a traditional funeral announcement.

It’s like a digital memory book + event planner + communication tool — all in one.

What does a Celebration of Life App typically do:
  • Event Planning: Set up the date, time, location, and details for a memorial or celebration
  • Memory Sharing: Upload and share photos, videos, stories, and memories
  • Guest Management: Send invites, manage RSVPs, share directions and event info
  • Personalization Tools: Choose songs, quotes, readings, and customize the event style
  • Keepsake Creation: Build digital guestbooks, memorial videos, printed programs
  • Virtual Participation: Allow remote guests to contribute memories online
  • Reminders & Notifications: Send event updates, memory submission reminders, or thank-you notes after the event

What are Common Features in Celebration of Life Apps:
  • Photo & Video Galleries: Create a digital scrapbook of the person’s life
  • Invitations & Announcements: Easily notify friends, family, and communities
  • Tribute Wall: Guests can post memories, condolences, and photos
  • Event Timeline: Outline the order of events (welcome, readings, music, etc.)
  • Private Family Area: Secure space for planning among close family members

Why do People Use Celebration of Life Apps:
  • Makes planning easier during an emotional time
  • Brings people together across distances
  • Creates a lasting memorial that lives beyond the event
  • Personalizes the experience to reflect the loved one’s life and passions
  • Streamlines communication (no missed invites or confusing logistics)

A celebration of life app turns the memory of a loved one into a shared, living experience — helping families and friends celebrate a life, not just mourn a loss.

What is a Cemetery Software Personal Workspace?
A cemetery software personal workspace is a public, customized area within cemetery management software where individual staff members — like sales counselors, grounds crew, or administrators — can view all tasks, activities, documents, and customer interactions.

It’s like a cemetery dashboard and task center for all employees inside the cemetery operation.

What does a Cemetery Personal Workspace typically include:
  • Task Lists: To-do items like customer follow-ups, work orders, maintenance jobs
  • Calendar: Public and personal view of appointments (burials, meetings, sales presentations)
  • Customer Activity: List of contacts assigned to them, showing recent interactions or needed follow-ups
  • Documents and Files: Quick access to personal contracts, service orders, permits they’re managing
  • Notifications: Personal reminders about upcoming services, payment due dates, project deadlines

What are Key Features of a Cemetery Software Personal Workspace:
  • Customizable Dashboard: See only the most important info for that staff member’s job
  • Quick Access to Records: Instantly pull up assigned contracts, plots, customer files
  • Calendar: Quick access to view and track all tasks, meetings, and services
  • Internal Messaging: Staff can communicate with each other inside the system without switching apps

Why is a Cemetery Personal Workspace important:
  • Keeps staff focused: Only shows tasks, customers, and work they’re responsible for — no distractions
  • Improves productivity: Quick access to personal priorities speeds up daily work
  • Increases accountability: Staff can easily track what they’ve done and what’s still pending
  • Enhances customer service: Faster responses and better attention to customer needs
  • Supports team collaboration: Each staff member works in sync but with their own priorities clear

​A cemetery software personal workspace gives each staff member their own focused command center, making daily operations smoother, faster, and more personalized.

What is Cemetery Software Help and Training?
Cemetery software self-help and training refers to the tools, resources, and systems that help cemetery staff learn how to use and implement the software, troubleshoot problems, and improve their skills — without always needing direct support from a vendor.

It's like having a virtual coach and knowledge library built into or alongside the cemetery software.

What does Cemetery Software Self-Help and Training typically include:
  • Knowledge Base: Articles, FAQs, how-to guides explaining features and workflows
  • Video Tutorials: Step-by-step videos showing how to complete common tasks (e.g., how to sell a plot or schedule a burial)
  • Interactive Walkthroughs: In-app popups or tours that guide users through tasks live inside the software
  • Troubleshooting Guides: Solutions for common problems (login issues, record corrections, plot mapping errors)
  • Printable Manuals and Cheat Sheets: Quick reference documents staff can print and keep at their desks
  • Training Courses: More structured learning paths with lessons and quizzes (basic, advanced, administrator training)
  • Live Chatbots or Help Desks: AI or staff-monitored chat support for instant help inside the software

What are some common Help Features?
  • Step-by-Step Checklists: Guides users through complex workflows like burial scheduling or plot sales
  • Training Webinars: Live or recorded classes on special topics (reporting, mapping, compliance)
  • Certification Programs: Optional badges for completing training modules (especially for administrators)

Why are Self-Help and Training Critical in Cemetery Software:
  • Reduces downtime: Staff can solve problems without waiting for IT or vendor support
  • Speeds up onboarding: New employees get up to speed faster
  • Boosts user confidence: Staff feel comfortable exploring more advanced features
  • Ensures consistency: Everyone follows standardized workflows
  • Improves software ROI: Better-trained staff get more value out of the software

Cemetery software self-help and training empowers cemetery staff to learn, troubleshoot, and improve at their own pace, ensuring the cemetery runs more smoothly with fewer headaches.
